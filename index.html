include maintenance personnel in the sampling scope of social responsibility audits

August 10, 2023 · 2 min read

The experience and measures taken by maintenance personnel are crucial in creating a safe and reliable working environment. These professionals have accumulated rich experience and knowledge in the maintenance and repair of machinery and equipment, enabling them to identify potential issues and take appropriate measures to prevent accidents and failures.

Firstly, maintenance personnel ensure that machinery and equipment are in good working condition through regular maintenance.​

They conduct routine inspections of various components, identify and address potential risks of failure, and ensure the normal operation of the machinery. This preventive maintenance work reduces the risks of unexpected shutdowns and production interruptions, while improving work efficiency and production stability.

Secondly, maintenance personnel shall prioritize the safety of machinery and equipment.​

They ensure that protective covers are intact and promptly repair or replace damaged parts. Additionally, they inspect and test the effectiveness of safety devices such as emergency stop buttons, ensuring that the machinery can be quickly stopped in emergency situations to protect the safety of operators.

Furthermore, maintenance personnel pay attention to the lubrication of machinery and equipment.​

They regularly check the usage of lubricants and add or replace them as needed. Proper lubrication reduces friction and wear of machinery, reduces noise generation, and extends the lifespan of the equipment. Adequate lubrication also helps to reduce the accumulation of heat caused by prolonged friction, thereby lowering the potential risks of fire and safety hazards.

Therefore, it is crucial to include maintenance personnel in the sampling scope of social responsibility audits.​

As guardians of machinery and equipment safety, they play a vital role in reducing the risks of equipment failures and potential harm through regular maintenance, lubrication, and the maintenance of safety devices. Their experience and practices are essential in assessing the safety of machinery and equipment.

tip

Hence, it is necessary to listen to their voices, understand their perspectives, and incorporate them into the scope of social responsibility audits. By doing so, we can ensure a comprehensive evaluation of the safety and reliability of the working environment.

BNW to BLW

July 31, 2023 · 2 min read

The concept of a Basic Living Wage (BLW) has been gaining traction in recent years as a way to ensure that all workers are able to meet their basic needs and live with dignity. However, i find several social audit reports , until now, still refer to the concept as the Basic Needs Wage (BNW), which was the earlier term used for this idea.

The Basic Needs Wage was first proposed in the 1970s by economist Karel Van Wolferen. The idea was to set a minimum wage that would cover the basic needs of a worker, including food, shelter, clothing, healthcare, and education. The idea was based on the principle that everyone has a right to a decent standard of living, and that a minimum wage should reflect this.

Over time, the concept of the Basic Needs Wage has evolved into the Basic Living Wage.

The Basic Living Wage is now seen as a more comprehensive approach to ensuring that workers can meet their basic needs. The idea is to set a wage that not only covers basic needs, but also allows for some discretionary spending, such as entertainment or savings.

The Basic Living Wage is intended to be a living wage, meaning that it should enable workers to live with dignity and participate fully in society. This means that the wage should be sufficient to cover not just the basic needs of the worker, but also those of their dependents, including children.

categorizing freedom of association under the complaints mechanism in IETP

July 30, 2023 · One min read

IETP has grouped employee representatives and freedom of association under Appeal Mechanism category, causing controversy.

Placing employee representatives and freedom of association under the "appeal mechanism" category is confusing their distinct purposes. Employee representatives are an organizational structure representing the collective will of employees, aiming to bring about positive changes through negotiation and communication. On the contrary, freedom of association is a right, allowing employees to form associations for protecting their collective interests, rather than being a mere avenue for appeals.

For instance, let's consider a situation where employee representatives propose increasing holiday gifts for workers to acknowledge their hard work over the past year. This proposal clearly aims to improve employee benefits and satisfaction rather than being a result of complaints or appeals. Employee representatives serve as legitimate channels to fight for employee welfare and rights, not just a mechanism for lodging complaints.

Placing "freedom of association" solely under the "complaints mechanism" could result in it receiving less emphasis and visibility, as the focus might primarily be on resolving individual complaints rather than upholding broader workers' rights.

Therefore, the classification of employee representatives alongside the appeal mechanism by IETP is questionable.

chaning rooms and shower facility for painting workers

July 25, 2023 · 2 min read

In the world of corporate social responsibility, there are certain areas that receive significant attention during audits and inspections, such as eye wash stations and shower facilities. These measures are put in place to ensure the safety and well-being of workers in industrial settings. However, there is one area that often gets overlooked - changing rooms and shower facilities.

In particular, workers who are involved in the painting process are exposed to harmful chemicals and substances on a daily basis. This not only poses a significant health risk, but it can also make for an uncomfortable work environment. After a long day of work, a worker's clothes and body can be covered in paint, leaving them feeling dirty and exposed to potential health hazards.

Providing adequate changing rooms and shower facilities is an essential part of creating a dignified work environment for employees. This not only ensures their physical safety but their emotional and mental well-being as well. It is important to recognize that employees are human beings with needs beyond just a paycheck. They deserve to be treated with respect and dignity, and to work in an environment that promotes their health and well-being.

it is imperative that companies recognize the importance of providing adequate changing rooms and shower facilities, especially in industries where workers are exposed to hazardous substances. This is not only a matter of social responsibility, but it is also a sound business practice that can benefit both employees and employers alike.
